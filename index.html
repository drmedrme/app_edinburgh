Festival shortlist board

Find the strongest daytime shows.

Use this board to coordinate Edinburgh Festival options in two-hour windows from 10:00 to 18:00, prioritising shows with five-star reviews, national coverage, known directors, and evidence of a strong previous festival run.

Priority filter

Known director or company, preferably five-star reviewed, reviewed by a credible outlet, and credible enough to plan a daytime slot around.

Working assumption

Some shows may return after a previous Edinburgh run because they reviewed so well. Treat that as a useful signal, then verify dates, venue, and current reviews before booking.

Core hours

Build the day from 10:00 to 18:00, leaving evenings open for meals, rest, or bigger ticketed events.

Fallback tab

Use Other Shows for strong reviews that miss one of the strict criteria, sit outside the daytime window, or need more checking.

Each window can hold one primary candidate plus a nearby backup if the venue timing is tight or tickets disappear.

Selection criteria

Use these signals to decide what belongs in the main shortlist.

  • 1Known director or companyPrioritise shows from a recognised director, company, writer, performer, or creative team.
  • 2Five-star reviewsPrefer shows with five-star reviews from credible publications or established reviewers.
  • 3Nationally reviewedLook for coverage from national newspapers, major arts publications, or widely read review outlets.
  • 4Returning or proven festival signalFlag shows that appear to have returned after a strong previous Edinburgh run or similar festival success.
  • 5Fits the daytime planMain candidates should run between 10:00 and 18:00 and fit cleanly into a two-hour planning window.
